Research Interest

My research interests are in computer cognition, robotics, machine learning, data analysis and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in the programming and design of accelerator to deal with massive data in quasi-real time.

Research Experience

I worked with Prof. Jerry Chou, focusing on distributed heterogeneous cluster. We found that sometimes HDD has advantage over SSD and vice versa. But HDDs are much cheaper than SSDs,so we want to propose a system that can perceive the type of incoming job by applying machine learning on the resource usage and then move the job to proper disk.

We harnessed Hadoop and analyze cluster resource usages (e.g. CPU time, I/O bandwidth) and apply machine learning to perceive the incoming job's nature (i.e. whether a I/O intensive, or a CPU intensive job, etc.) and finally move the computing to the most suitable disk.

This system can maximize the resource management of company data center and lower the price of building one.

I also worked with worked with Prof. Min Sun, specializing in image analysis. In Taiwan, or even in the whole world, Google Street View (GSV) is not updated frequently enough. Our group therefore ultimately want to construct a system that mitigate the above issue by processing dashcam video.
