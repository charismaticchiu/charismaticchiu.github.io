About

During my time at NTHU, I was the vice president and Cello principal of NTHU orchestra, chanselor of NTHU student council and an activist of Tsinghua residential college. I am also a member of Phi Tau Phi Honorary Society.

Research Interest

My research interests are in computer cognition, robotics, machine learning, data analysis and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in the programming and design of accelerator to deal with massive data in quasi-real time.

Research Experience

Large-Scale System Architecture Lab.


I worked with Prof. Jerry Chou, focusing on distributed heterogeneous cluster. We found that on different circumstances, HDD has advantage over SSD and vice versa. But HDDs are much cheaper than SSDs; thus we want to propose a system that can perceive the type of incoming job by applying machine learning on the resource usage and then move the job to proper disk.

We harnessed Hadoop and analyzed cluster resource usages (e.g. CPU time, I/O bandwidth, etc.) and tried to perceive the incoming job's nature (i.e. whether it is an I/O intensive, or a CPU intensive job, etc.) and finally moved the computing to the most suitable disk.

This system can maximize the resource management of company data center and lower the price of building one.

Visual Science Lab.


I also worked with worked with Prof. Min Sun, specializing in image analysis. In Taiwan, or even in the whole world, Google Street View (GSV) is not updated frequently enough. Our group therefore ultimately would like to construct a system that mitigate the above issue by processing dashcam video. Briefly speaking, we segmented videos of customer-level dashboard camera into images, then conducted change detections with GSV and eventually proceeded to update the differences. Results of this project was submitted to IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo (ICME) 2016.
